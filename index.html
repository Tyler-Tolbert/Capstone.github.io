About

This is a presentation based on the research and findings of 3 critial communication skills. Listening, Non-Verbal, and Conversation.

Listening

Listening is the ability to receive and interpret messages in the communication process.

Listening is the key to all effective communication. Without this ability to listen, messages are easily misunderstood. This could result in communication mistakes and the sender of the message can easily become frustrated or irritated.

Listening is so important that many top employers provide listening skills training for their employees. Many successful leaders and entrepreneurs credit their success to effective listening skills.

This is not surprising when you consider that good listening skills can lead to better customer satisfaction, greater productivity with fewer mistakes, and increased sharing of information that in turn can lead to more creative and innovative work.

Benefits of Listening:

Friends

Gain a greater number of friends both in social media and IRL. It could also improve self-esteem and confidence.

Grades

Obtain higher grades at school and in academic work and even better health and general well-being.

Health

Studies have shown that, whereas speaking raises blood pressure, attentive listening can bring the blood pressure down.

Non-Verbal

One skill that is needed is listening. Starting with silence. Because nature abhors a vacuum, silence is a very powerful communication-stimulating skill.

A professional never wants to be too silent, because that gives off like they are not listening. What they should do is use “minimal verbal encouragers. Examples include “I see,” “Go on,” “What else?” and “Please tell me more.

Questions to Avoid:

"Yes/No"

“Do you have anything else that I should know today?” it would be easy for a grieving client to distractedly answer, “No.” Instead, you will obtain more useful information by asking, “What else do you want me to know today?”

"Why?"

Avoid asking “why questions. Asking “Why” seems to question a client’s motivation.

"I..."

Lastly, avoiding “trite or canned expressions. Statements such as, “I understand how you feel” increase the risk of the client’s saying or thinking, “How can you understand? Have you ever had a husband killed by a drunk driver?

A professional needs the skill of jargon and legalese. The last thing a professional wants to do is give false hope or make the client think that something is wrong with the way they are feeling and cannot handle the situation by themselves.

Professionals need to avoid “premature advice. “How about seeing a grief counselor?” Lastly avoiding “false reassurance. “Don’t worry, I will take care of everything.”

Conversation

A conversation is a consistent exchange of message between participants involved in joint operations and thus, have common interest. With this in mind, one must ask the question, how do web services help humans in their ever day conversations?

A web service is an accessible application that other applications and people as well can find and discover and satisfy the needs of themselves, and others around the world.

The enhancement of Web services with new mechanism is backed by Hanson Et al, who claimed that “But a present, Web services remain a “vending machine” model, that is that they are the limit themselves to providing a way in which functions can be make available for invocation over the internet. Rich e-business interactions require a more peer to peer proactive, dynamic, loosely-coupled mode of interaction.”

In the study, they classified four mechanisms, Domain-Dependent, Domain-Independent, Composition-Driven, and Execution-Driven.

Domain

Domain-Dependent conversation mechanisms deal with the features of the application domain when it comes for example to structuring the format and content of the conversation messages to exchange. The opposite occurs for Domain-Independent.

Composition

The Composition-Driven conversations represent the conversations that are needed to devise a composite service such as how to look for the web services and how to exchange agreements between these web services.

Execution

Execution-Driven conversation mechanisms represent the messages that are needed to deploy a composite service. Therefore, the chronology of conversations starts with the composition-driven and ends with execution-driven conversation.
